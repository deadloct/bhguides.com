Strategy

Kill order: Flurgie/Skuttlez/Candelabros, then Findle, then others.

Kris Gemmgle's healing is fantastic for a DPS, and its freeze was definitely beneficial as well. With 3+ enemies, I would alternate between its 0sp and its 3sp AOE freeze skill that gives 1sp to the team. Once the enemies dropped to only 1 or 2, I'd swap the 3sp AOE for the 2sp freeze random and give 1sp to random ally. I rarely used other skills.

Hoplag was a compromise. I didn't have this event's block tank — Tifology — fully stabled yet, and my only other leg tank is Googie. With only 1 legendary evade pump, I decided to equip all my block augments to it for a total of 78.6% block. This ended up working out!

Hoplag is a surprisingly good tank with an excellent skillset. I usually alternated between its 0sp and the 3sp spread heal for when things were getting dicey. The 4sp is also a fantastic nuke, but I only used it once against a boss when I had a surplus of shield and full health.

For my own skills, I mainly used Harvester's 1sp AOE against teams of 2+ to trigger Zarli, my healing pet. When down to one enemy, I either spammed my 0sp to keep my healing skill open or I would use the 1sp target attack. Often I'd end up with a fair amount of SP because of Kris Gemmgle's SP boosting skills.

The dungeon is pretty straightforward, but I still had to use 2x super potions during my winning run. This camper of mine is pretty well equipped, but it's still missing a few mythic items, and my familiars have pretty weak augments.
